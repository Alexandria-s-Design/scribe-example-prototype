### **Why RSCSE is NOT Recommended for This Study:**

Even though permission can likely be obtained, we **strongly recommend against using RSCSE** for Dorthia's project because:

1. **Wrong Population:** RSCSE was designed for **family caregivers** of cognitively impaired older adults, NOT professional healthcare workers

2. **Domain Mismatch:** The three subscales measure:
   - Obtaining Respite (not relevant to paid professionals)
   - Responding to Disruptive Patient Behaviors (specific to dementia care)
   - Controlling Upsetting Thoughts (more relevant to unpaid caregivers)

3. **Face Validity Issues:** Faculty reviewers will question why a family caregiver instrument is being used for professional staff

4. **Conceptual Misalignment:** Care coordinators face different challenges:
   - Reaching members by phone
   - Documenting encounters
   - Navigating healthcare systems
   - Meeting productivity standards

   These are NOT assessed by RSCSE.

## üìù **DOCUMENTATION FOR DNP PROJECT**

### **What to Include in Feasibility Report:**

**Section: Instruments to Measure Outcomes**

**Instrument 1: General Self-Efficacy Scale (GSE)**

*Purpose:* Measure care coordinator self-efficacy before and after protocol implementation

*Description:* The GSE is a 10-item psychometric scale designed to assess general self-efficacy in professional contexts (Schwarzer & Jerusalem, 1995). Sample items include "I can always manage to solve difficult problems if I try hard enough" and "I can usually handle whatever comes my way."

*Psychometric Properties:*
- Internal consistency: Cronbach's Œ± = 0.76-0.90 across populations
- Test-retest reliability: r = 0.47-0.75 over 1-6 months
- Construct validity: Correlates with work satisfaction (r = 0.38), optimism (r = 0.52), and negatively with anxiety (r = -0.37)

*Scoring:* Total scores range from 10-40; higher scores indicate greater self-efficacy

*Administration:* Anonymous online survey (REDCap or Qualtrics) at baseline (Week 1) and post-intervention (Month 3)

*Permission:* The GSE is freely available for research purposes; no formal permission required (Schwarzer, 2014)

**References:**

Luszczynska, A., Scholz, U., & Schwarzer, R. (2005). The General Self-Efficacy Scale: Multicultural validation studies. *The Journal of Psychology, 139*(5), 439-457. https://doi.org/10.3200/JRLP.139.5.439-457

Schwarzer, R. (2014). *Self-efficacy: Thought control of action*. Routledge.

Schwarzer, R., & Jerusalem, M. (1995). Generalized Self-Efficacy scale. In J. Weinman, S. Wright, & M. Johnston (Eds.), *Measures in health psychology: A user's portfolio. Causal and control beliefs* (pp. 35-37). NFER-NELSON.
