### 1. **APA Revised Scale for Caregiving Self-Efficacy**

#### ‚úÖ **HIGHLY APPROPRIATE FOR GRADUATE RESEARCH**

**Original Study:**
- Steffen, A. M., McKibbin, C., Zeiss, A. M., & Gallagher-Thompson, D. (2002). The Revised Scale for Caregiving Self-Efficacy: Reliability and Validity Studies. *The Journals of Gerontology: Series B, 57*(1), P74‚ÄìP86.

**Psychometric Properties:**
- **Strong internal consistency** across all 3 subscales
- **Adequate test-retest reliability**
- **Construct validity** supported by relationships with depression, anxiety, anger, perceived social support, and criticism
- **Two large validation samples**: Study 1 (N=169), Study 2 (N=145)

**Cross-National Validation:**
- **58 peer-reviewed studies** validate the scale worldwide
- Validated in **6 languages**: Arabic, Chinese, English, French, Italian, Spanish
- 72% of studies used non-English translations successfully
- Considered the **"gold standard"** for caregiver self-efficacy measurement

**Three Domains Measured:**
1. **Obtaining Respite**
2. **Responding to Disruptive Patient Behaviors**
3. **Controlling Upsetting Thoughts**

**Graduate Research Use:**
- ‚úÖ **Widely used in doctoral dissertations and master's theses**
- ‚úÖ **No restrictions on educational use**
- ‚úÖ **Sensitivity to change demonstrated** (critical for QI projects)
- ‚úÖ **Available through APA website** with scoring instructions

**Relevance to Dorthia's Project:**
The scale measures caregiver self-efficacy, but Dorthia's staff are **care coordinators**, not family caregivers. This requires justification for adapting the instrument or selecting a more appropriate measure.

**‚ö†Ô∏è CONSIDERATION:** The scale was designed for **family caregivers of cognitively impaired older adults**, NOT professional healthcare workers. While psychometrically sound, you'll need to justify its application to professional care coordination staff.

### 2. **First Call Resolution (FCR)**

#### ‚ö†Ô∏è **USE WITH CAUTION - VALIDITY CONCERNS**

**Definition:**
- Contact center metric measuring percentage of customer issues resolved on first interaction
- Healthcare industry average: **71-72% FCR rate**

**Validity Concerns:**
1. **No industry standard** for internal FCR measurement
2. **Measurement variance** of 10-20% depending on method:
   - Internal measurement (call logs): Often **overstates by 10-20%**
   - External measurement (post-call surveys): More accurate but resource-intensive
3. **Definition inconsistency**: "Resolved" varies by organization
   - Some define as "no transfer occurred"
   - Others require "no follow-up work needed"

**Recommended Measurement Method:**
- **Voice of the Customer (VoC) post-call survey** = most accurate
- Let customers judge whether FCR occurred
- 70% of healthcare call centers using post-call surveys show year-over-year improvement

**Healthcare-Specific Data:**
- Relevant for **health insurance call centers**
- Linked to **patient satisfaction** and **reduced call volume**
- Associated with **lower operational costs**

**Graduate Research Use:**
- ‚úÖ **Acceptable as an operational metric**
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Must clearly define** what constitutes "resolution"
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Must specify measurement method** (internal vs. external)
- ‚úÖ **Can be triangulated** with other engagement metrics
- ‚ö†Ô∏è **Limited peer-reviewed validation** as a research instrument

**Key References:**
- SQM Group. (2020). *FCR Metric Operating Philosophy: A Comprehensive Guide*.
- American Health Connection. (2023). *The Importance of First Call Resolution in Healthcare Call Centers*.

**Concerns:**
1. Family caregivers ‚â† Professional healthcare workers

**Potential Solutions:**
1. **Adapt the scale** with expert review (requires validation)

   - **General Self-Efficacy Scale (GSE)** (Schwarzer & Jerusalem, 1995)

### Step 2: Select and Justify Instruments (Week 1-2)

#### For **Staff Self-Efficacy** (if measuring staff):

**Recommended Instrument:** **General Self-Efficacy Scale (GSE)** by Schwarzer & Jerusalem (1995)

**Why GSE Instead of APA Caregiving Scale?**
- ‚úÖ Designed for **working adults in professional contexts**
- ‚úÖ **10 items, 4-point Likert scale** (quick to administer)
- ‚úÖ **Validated in 33+ countries** and 28+ languages
- ‚úÖ **Cronbach's alpha: 0.76-0.90** (excellent reliability)
- ‚úÖ **Widely used in healthcare worker studies**
- ‚úÖ **Free to use** for research purposes
- ‚úÖ **Sensitive to intervention effects**

**Citation:**
- Schwarzer, R., & Jerusalem, M. (1995). Generalized Self-Efficacy scale. In J. Weinman, S. Wright, & M. Johnston (Eds.), *Measures in health psychology: A user's portfolio. Causal and control beliefs* (pp. 35-37). Windsor, UK: NFER-NELSON.

**Alternative (if you want domain-specific):**
Create a **brief 5-item domain-specific scale** based on the 3 critical tasks:
1. I am confident in my ability to **reach members on the first attempt**.
2. I am confident in my ability to **verify and update member contact information accurately**.
3. I am confident in my ability to **engage members in care coordination services**.
4. I am confident in my ability to **document outreach attempts according to protocol**.
5. I am confident in my ability to **follow up with members who are initially unreachable**.

- **Rated on 5-point Likert scale**: 1 (Not at all confident) to 5 (Extremely confident)
- Administer **pre-intervention** and **post-intervention** (at 3 months)
- Calculate **total score** (5-25) and **mean score** (1-5)

---

#### For **First Call Resolution (FCR)**:

**Operational Definition Required:**

**Recommended Definition:**
> "First Call Resolution (FCR) is defined as successful member contact and engagement in care coordination within the first outreach attempt, confirmed by one of the following:
> 1. Member verbally agrees to participate in care coordination
> 2. Care coordinator schedules initial care planning appointment
> 3. Member contact information is verified and updated as needed
> 4. No additional outreach attempts required within 7 days due to member responsiveness"

**Measurement Method:**
1. **Primary:** Electronic care management system logs
   - Track: Date/time of first attempt, outcome code, follow-up needed (Y/N)
2. **Secondary:** Brief post-call member survey (optional, for validation)
   - "Did the care coordinator answer all your questions during this call?" (Yes/No)
   - "Do you need to call back for additional information?" (Yes/No)

**Data Collection:**
- Calculate **FCR rate** = (# of first-call resolutions / # of total first-attempt calls) √ó 100
- Measure **weekly** and report **monthly** trends
- Compare **baseline period** (June-August 2025) vs. **intervention period** (implementation + 3 months)
