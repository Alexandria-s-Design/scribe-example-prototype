The Isolation of Innovation

Teaching science, especially complex topics like systems biology, can sometimes feel like navigating a maze alone. Traditional textbooks often fall short, presenting static diagrams that fail to capture the dynamic interactions within living systems. This leaves you, the teacher, to bridge the gap, spending countless hours crafting engaging lessons and activities. The challenge is real: how do you make abstract concepts accessible and exciting for students who are used to interactive, digital experiences? And how do you find the time to do it all?
